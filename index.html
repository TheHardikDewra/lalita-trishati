The Lalita Trishati is a sacred hymn from the Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa, revealed by Lord Hayagrīva to the sage Agastya at the express command of the Divine Mother herself. It contains 300 names of Lalitā Tripurasundarī, arranged in 59 verses across three sections. While the Lalitā Sahasranāma is described as the fragrance of the flower, the Trishati is the flower itself - more secret, more compact, the Goddess's own wealth. The text is described in its own phala śruti as sarva-pūrti-karī - the fulfiller of all completeness.

Name of the Day

From the Uttarapīṭhikā, Hayagrīva's teaching to Agastya:

यः कीर्तयेत्सकृत्भक्त्या दिव्य-नामशत-त्रयम् ।
तस्य पुण्यमहं वक्ष्ये श‍ृणु त्वं कुम्भसम्भव ॥
yaḥ kīrtayet sakṛt bhaktyā divya-nāma-śata-trayam | tasya puṇyam ahaṃ vakṣye śṛṇu tvaṃ kumbha-sambhava ||
"Whoever, with devotion, recites these three hundred divine names even once - hear, O pot-born sage, the merit they accrue."
रहस्यनाम-साहस्रपाठे यत्फलमीरितम् ।
तत्फलं कोटिगुणितमेक-नामजपाद्भवेत् ॥
rahasya-nāma-sāhasra-pāṭhe yat phalam īritam | tat phalaṃ koṭi-guṇitam eka-nāma-japād bhavet ||
"The merit declared for reciting the secret thousand names is multiplied ten-million-fold by the recitation of even a single name from this Trishati."
आयुष्करं पुष्टिकरं पुत्रदं वश्यकारकम् ।
विद्याप्रदं कीर्तिकरं सुकवित्व-प्रदायकम् ॥
सर्वसम्पत्प्रदं सर्वभोगदं सर्वसौख्यदम् ।
सर्वाभीष्टप्रदं चैव देव्या नामशतत्रयम् ॥
"It bestows long life, vigour, children, mastery; it grants knowledge, fame, and poetic genius; it confers all wealth, all enjoyments, all happiness, and the fulfilment of every desire - this triple-hundred of names of the Goddess."
॥ इति श्रीब्रह्माण्डपुराणे उत्तरखण्डे श्रीहयग्रीवागस्त्य-संवादे श्रीललितात्रिशती-स्तोत्रकथनं सम्पूर्णम् ॥
Thus ends the narration of the Sri Lalita Trishati Stotra, from the dialogue between Sri Hayagriva and Agastya, in the Uttarakhanda of the Brahmanda Purana.

Tradition holds that recitation on Fridays renders the veil between devotee and Devī almost transparent. The text is described as sarva-pūrti-karī (the fulfiller of all completeness) and rahasyāti-rahasya (secret beyond all secrets). Chanted with sincerity, it bridges bhoga (worldly fulfilment) and mokṣa (liberation).
